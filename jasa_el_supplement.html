Abstract

Accent plays a crucial role in speaker identity and broad utility of speech technologies. Existing accented Text-to-Speech (TTS) systems either require large-scale accented datasets or lack fine-grained phoneme-level controllability. The present work describes an accented TTS framework that combines coarse phonological rules with a pretrained multilingual TTS model, requiring no accented training data and no target-accent speaker embeddings. Additionally, the trade-off between explicit phoneme-level duration alignment and natural language-specific timing is examined. Experimental results demonstrate effective accent shifts while maintaining speech quality and perceptual naturalness, showing that combining knowledge-based phonological transformations with native-language speaker embeddings can generate controllable L2-accented English.
